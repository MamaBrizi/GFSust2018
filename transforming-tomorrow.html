Transforming Tomorrow

Looking ahead, we expect to achieve measurable progress toward the following sustainability goals and actions.

Reduce Energy Usage by 15%

We regularly improve the performance of the facilities we own in Harrisburg and Valley Forge through capital investments and routine equipment upgrades. In 2019, the Corporate Sustainability Team will launch a desktop study to reduce energy usage by 15% with a 10-year buyback goal at these two facilities. The study will include renewable energy options, building management technologies, such as smart building capabilities, phased implementation, maintenance, and future expansion and upgrades.
Increase Community Involvement

We support our communities, promote collaboration, and provide opportunities for community service in various ways. An initiative to develop and deploy a robust community involvement program is on the horizon. We'll focus on clear objectives, annual goals, and reporting to unify employees around their community service efforts.
Prepare for the Future

What four or five external forces are most likely to "disrupt" the progression of sustainability throughout the world 10 years from now? How will businesses, the industry, and society at large be impacted? These are questions that Gannett Fleming and other industry-leading organizations are exploring as part of a new project with The Global Environmental Management Initiative (GEMI), in conjunction with the Institute for the Future (IFTF). The goal of the project is to use the foresight to help GEMI members prepare for the future and develop better-informed business strategies and approaches now and for the future.

GEMI is the global leader in developing insights, networking, and creating collaborative sustainability solutions for business. Gannett Fleming, a GEMI member since 2015, is the only engineering firm of the 17-member organizations. Mark Johnson, a Gannett Fleming senior vice president and vice-chair of the GEMI board, will be part of the project, which launched in early 2019. Johnson previously served as corporate sustainability officer for Gannett Fleming.
