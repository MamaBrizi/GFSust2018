Tracking Progress

We are turning our energy and carbon levels down through the decisions we make every day, from switching to LED lights and replacing aging building equipment with more efficient units to recycling obsolete technology. Every action or decision can contribute to our larger effort to protect our natural resources and reduce our environmental impacts.

We know we are making a difference through our annual gut check to analyze and report our utility usage data at the buildings we own—three buildings at our corporate headquarters in Harrisburg, Pennsylvania, and an office in Valley Forge, Pennsylvania.

We evaluated usage changes from 2017 to 2018 and compared usage in the current year against our 2010 baseline, which is the average of data from 2007, 2008, and 2009. Although our consumption increased slightly from 2017 to 2018, our overall results were below the baseline.

Capital investments and routine equipment upgrades at these facilities have contributed to the long-term decline. In 2018, renovations of our headquarters lobby and other office spaces throughout the campus incorporate our standards for low volatile organic compound paint, recycled content in furniture and flooring, and occupancy sensors that detect activity and automatically turn lights on and off, saving electricity.

Headquarters Recycling Program

Against the backdrop of successes is a setback in our recycling program. Our Harrisburg recycling provider simplified the list of items that may be recycled to aluminum and steel cans, plastic or glass bottles and jugs, newspaper, cardboard, and milk cartons—eliminating recycling of mixed-use paper and magazines, among other items. The new guidelines reflect the global downturn in the recyclables market.

Still, our white office paper recycling program remains strong. In 2018, Harrisburg employees recycled 87,332 pounds of white office paper, which returned a $2,789 rebate from our recycling vendor. Since 2011, we earned $21,373 from our rebate program. White office paper is a valuable recycling product, as it retains much of its value while reprocessing into other products.

Carbon Footprint Reductions

Annually, we evaluate the carbon footprint generated by our three Harrisburg campus buildings from electrical and natural gas usage. Greenhouse gas emissions from natural gas combustion slightly increased between 2017 and 2018, from 265 CO2e tons to 290 CO2e tons, respectively. However, compared to the baseline, we have reduced greenhouse gas emissions in this category by 25%.Greenhouse gas emissions from electrical usage show a 26% decrease from the baseline. Between 2017 and 2018, we increased emissions by 9%, emitting 1,449 tons of CO2e in 2017, compared to 1,579 CO2e tons in 2018.

Go Green Group

We created a Go Green Group on our internal enterprise social network site to share and encourage sustainable behaviors. Stewart Vaghti, corporate sustainability officer, and Sandra Hansen, sustainability coordinator for our Phoenix, Arizona, office, manage the site, in which employees post stories and examples of sustainability happenings throughout the company.

We were inspired to create the site based on our participation in the Trip Reduction Program (TRP) through Maricopa County and Valley Metro, a regional public transportation agency serving residents of metro Phoenix. Employers who participate in the program pledge to implement and promote travel reduction measures to reduce traffic congestion and improve air quality. While the site began with our Phoenix office staff involved in the TRP, we opened it companywide, recognizing that sharing our stories builds our culture of sustainability.

Smart Paper Purchasing

Gannett Fleming has traditionally used paper with recycled content. In 2018, for the first time, we tracked the eco-savings of this practice for all Gannett Fleming domestic offices, thanks to a new partnership with a national reprographics services company. Our agreement with the print and document management provider enables us to capture the environmental benefits of using paper with 30% recycled content compared to non-recycled paper.

Strategic Sourcing

We launched a strategic sourcing program in mid-2013, contracting with a large national retailer to handle our procurement of office supplies, cleaning products, office furniture, shipping, and equipment. The goal of the program is to realize cost reductions from contractual pricing, reduce our administrative burden, eliminate unnecessary office supply purchases and waste, and improve the way we monitor our cleaning product purchases. Here are highlights from 2018.

• Total orders below $50 dropped slightly from 76 in 2017 to 74 in 2018—movement in line with our goal to reduce environmental and economic impacts by consolidating orders. • The percentage of eco-rated products was down slightly from 2018, but still hovering around 30%. • Total orders jumped 21%. Part of the increase may reflect of our 12% increase in employees from 2017 to 2018.

Mileage Impacts

For a comprehensive look at CO2e emissions from vehicle travel companywide, we considered mileage from personal vehicles, rental cars, and corporate fleet vehicles. We normalized the data against our annual firm revenue and number of employees—two data points that have steadily increased since the baseline 2010 year. Between 2017 and 2018, our employee count increased by 8% and our CO2e emissions per employee increased 12%. A sharp year-to-year 21% increase in total vehicle miles traveled contributed to the increases.
