Think Globally, Act Locally

Working to create stronger and more sustainable local communities is an effective way to make the world a better place for everyone. We are proud to share a few ways we made a difference in 2018.

Go Red for Women

Ten women from the Harrisburg office participated in the American Heart Association and American Stroke Association's Go Red for Women Luncheon. The event promotes awareness on how heart disease affects women, educates health care providers so that they treat women according to guidelines, and raises funds for research on women's heart health.

Client Collaboration

The Virginia Beach office partnered with HRSD, a Gannett Fleming client, to host a fundraiser for the Wounded Warrior Project, which provides a variety of important services for military veterans.

Support of WTS Scholarship

Employees from Pennsylvania offices supported the Women's Transportation Seminar at the second annual Scholarship Gala and Fundraiser, held in Pittsburgh. The event featured a presentation by Katrina Ricks, the city's director of mobility and infrastructure.

New Heights

The Toronto in Ontario, Canada, office contributed to the United Way through sponsorships and by participating in the Metrolinx United Way Golf Tournament. Toronto employees also climbed the CN Tower's 144 flights of stairs as part of a United Way fundraiser.

Milk and Honey

Harrisburg employees painted a chicken coop and completed other construction work for Milk & Honey Farms in Linglestown, Pennsylvania. The nonprofit farm donates food to shelters and food banks around the Harrisburg area. The work was part of an International Bridge, Tunnel and Turnpike Association (IBTTA) Foundation's service project.

Teaching Sustainability

Three Pittsburgh office employees, Bob Dengler, PE, project manager; Kassandra Kimmey; and Bob Sciullo, PE, construction manager, volunteered at the Allegheny County Sanitary Authority's Annual Open House, an environmental festival that teaches about sustainability through engineering and science games designed to appeal to all ages.

Dress for Success

Members of Gannett Fleming's Connected Women employee resource group in the Phoenix office participated in a "Diva Dash," a high-heel relay race, as part of Heels for Healing, a program that provides women services such as safe housing, education, health care, counseling, and independent living skills. Connected Women groups in the Los Angeles, Raleigh, and Harrisburg offices led clothing drives in support of Dress for Success charities, which provide a network of support, professional attire, and development tools to empower women entering the workforce.

Science Vouchers

Benjamin Blocher, ENV SP, WPIT, an environmental scientist in the Baltimore office, and Laura Vrabel, PWS, a project environmental scientist from Harrisburg, volunteered at the annual USA Science & Engineering Festival in Washington, D.C. Thousands of visitors from across the country attended the event to learn about all aspects of STEM education.

Giving Back to Schools

Many offices participated in back-to-school fundraising drives for students and teachers. In support of the effort, Paul Nowicki, PE, president and COO, said, "Gannett Fleming is committed to the communities where we live and work. The donations distributed to these schools will ensure the students and teachers have the essential supplies they need to be successful." More than 3,000 supplies were donated.

Community Civility Counts

Anthony Burnett Sr., from the Harrisburg office, received the World Civility Award from Community Civility Counts during its third annual World Civility Day event. World Civility Day honors the example of living civil in our world and highlights the good work of those who help others improve their lives.

Family Lunches

The Raleigh office purchased, cooked, and served lunch to local Ronald McDonald House families in Durham during a day of service. Ronald McDonald House® offers care and resources to support families of children with serious illnesses.

A Strong Workforce

Pennsylvania Gov. Tom Wolf appointed Bob Scaer, PE, chairman and CEO, to Pennsylvania's Workforce Development Board. Scaer joins 46 others who advise the governor on building a strong workforce development system aligned with state education and economic development goals.

Industry Leaders

Gannett Fleming and the Orange County, California, chapter of the American Society of Civil Engineers co-sponsored "The Future is Female–Championing Women Engineers," an interactive roundtable discussion. Aileen Santos-Redman, PE, Los Angeles office structural lead and a Connected Ambassador for the Connected Women initiative, served as moderator for the panel, which included DOT & Tolls Southern California Area Manager Derya Thompson and other industry leaders.
