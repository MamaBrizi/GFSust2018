Automated Vehicle Coalition Forms; New Legislation Passes

Automated vehicles (AV) that communicate with infrastructure and each other have the potential to prevent or reduce the impact from millions of accidents each year. The U.S. Department of Transportation National Highway Traffic Safety Administration reports that 95 percent of road crashes are caused by human error.

Confident in technology's ability to reduce crashes and save lives, Gannett Fleming became a founding, charter member of the Automated Vehicle Coalition (AVC), which formed in April 2018. The coalition addresses technology, operations, and policy issues that impact the development of safe, secure, and automated mobility. Including members from industry, government, and academia, it is committed to sharing insights and best practices. The AVC strives to accelerate private sector innovation, reduce safety risks, and improve roads and communities. The organization's relationships with lawmakers, businesses, and civic organizations enable it to advance these potentially life-saving technologies.

In 2018, the AVC, in partnership with Gannett Fleming, had a strategic role in supporting groundbreaking legislation that expanded automated vehicle laws in the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania. A new law amends Title 75, allowing the Pennsylvania Department of Transportation and Pennsylvania Turnpike Commission to deploy driverless crash trucks on highway construction sites. Traditionally, workers would operate these trucks in active construction zones with the sole purpose of absorbing vehicle collisions to protect others working on the site. The new law, now Act 117, also allows for the platooning of motor vehicles with assistive driving technology.

Leaders in the public and private sectors gathered at Gannett Fleming's headquarters in Harrisburg on Nov. 20, 2018, for a press conference celebrating the passage of the legislation and its role in protecting highway workers, advancing the efficient movement of goods and freight, and promoting traveler safety.
